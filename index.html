How to Interpret Correlations

Pearson Correlation Coefficient (r) ranges from -1.0 to +1.0. It quantifies the strength and direction of the linear relationship between application features and their predicted rating outcomes.

Positive Coefficient (+r): Indicates that as the attribute increases, the application rating tends to increase as well (e.g. higher review engagement ratios or active updates).

Negative Coefficient (-r): Indicates that as the attribute increases, the rating tends to decrease (e.g. longer stagnation cycles, bloated file sizes, or aggressive ad modules).

Practical Interpretation: Features with values closer to +1.0 or -1.0 represent the strongest levers for your product strategy. Focus on maximizing positive attributes and strictly eliminating negative drag factors to maximize storefront standing.
